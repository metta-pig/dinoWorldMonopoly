# Dino World Monopoly

_A kid-friendly roll-and-move park game for ages 7–10. Original theme — not affiliated with Hasbro Monopoly._

## Overview

2–4 players explore a prehistoric park, buy dino habitats, charge visit fees, and build nests and exhibits. Last explorer with cash wins!

**Time:** about 30–40 minutes  
**You need:** board, habitat cards, Fossil Find and Park Event cards, Dino Bucks ($1, $2, $5 bills), two dice, tokens, nest and exhibit markers.

---

## Setup

1. Place the board where everyone can reach it.
2. Shuffle **Fossil Find** and **Park Event** cards face-down beside the board.
3. Each player picks a token and starts on the **Hatchery** (space 0).
4. The bank gives each player **$12** in Dino Bucks (mix of $1, $2, and $5 bills).
5. Youngest player goes first; play moves **clockwise** around the table.

---

## On your turn

### 1. Roll and move

The active player rolls **two dice** and moves that many spaces **clockwise**.

- If you pass the Hatchery (even if you don’t land on it), collect **$2**.
- **Optional adult rule:** rolling **doubles** lets you roll again once; three doubles in a row sends you to the Tar Pit.

### 2. Resolve the space

| Space | What happens |
|-------|----------------|
| **Empty habitat** | You may **buy** it for the price on the card, or **pass**. No auction — it stays with the bank. |
| **Someone else’s habitat** | Pay the **visit fee** on their card to that player. |
| **Your habitat** | Nothing extra. |
| **Fossil Find** | Draw top card, do what it says, return it (unless it says to keep it). |
| **Park Event** | Draw top Park Event card, do what it says. |
| **Visitor Center Fee** | Pay **$2** to the bank. |
| **Migration Route** | Same as a habitat — buy if empty ($4), pay visit fee if owned. |
| **Tar Pit** | See **Tar Pit** below. |
| **Free Digging** | Rest — nothing happens. |
| **Ranger Outpost** | Go straight to the **Tar Pit**. Do **not** collect $2 if you pass the Hatchery. |

### 3. Build (optional)

After moving, you may build on habitats you own **if you own every habitat in that biome**:

- **Nest** — costs **$1**. Max **2** nests per habitat.
- **Exhibit** — costs **$3**. You must have 2 nests first; the exhibit replaces them.

**Even building rule:** add one nest at a time across habitats in the same biome before adding a second nest on any of them.

Example: You own all three Forest habitats. Add 1 nest on Stego Meadow, then 1 on Triceratops Grove, then 1 on Brachiosaurus Heights — then you may add second nests.

### 4. End turn

Hand the dice to the next player.

---

## Visit fees (rent)

Each habitat card shows visit fees. If you own **every habitat in a biome**, double the **base** fee (before counting nests).

**Migration routes:** 1 route owned → **$1**; both routes → **$2**.

## Tar Pit

- **Just visiting** (you landed there on a normal roll): nothing bad — wait for your next turn.
- **Sent to Tar Pit** (Ranger Outpost or card): do **not** collect Hatchery money on the way.
- **Your turn in the Tar Pit:** pay **$1** to leave **or** roll **doubles** to leave for free.
- **Get Out of Tar Pit** card: play on your turn instead of paying; follow the card, then roll and move.

---

## Bankruptcy

If you owe money and cannot pay (even after selling is not allowed in this game — no mortgages), you are **bankrupt**:

- Give all your habitats and routes back to the bank (remove nests and exhibits).
- You are out of the game.

---

## Winning

When only **one** player is left, that player wins!

**Classroom time limit:** if time runs out, everyone adds cash + habitat/route prices; highest total wins.
