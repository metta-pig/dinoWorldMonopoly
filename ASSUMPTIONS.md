# Assumptions

Decisions made where the brief was open, plus complexity flags for ages 7–10.

## Resolved from brief

- **9 habitats** on a **20-space** track (Monopoly Junior scale, not 22-property classic).
- **Biome grouping** (Swamp / Desert / Forest / Ice) instead of geologic eras — easier to color and explain to kids.
- **Two Migration Routes** (not four railroads) to shorten play and reduce math.
- **No utilities** in v1.
- **Hot-seat digital** only; no network multiplayer.

## Proposed for ages 7–10

| Topic | Choice | Rationale |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Starting cash | **$12** | Small bills only; enough to buy several $1–$2 habitats |
| Hatchery bonus | **$2** | Easy to remember; keeps cash flowing |
| Habitat prices | **$1–$5** | Kid-friendly mental math (not $100–$200 scale) |
| Nest cost | **$1** each, max **2** per habitat | Matches low-dollar economy |
| Exhibit cost | **$3**, replaces nests | One clear upgrade step |
| Tar Pit | Pay **$1** or roll **doubles** to leave | No 3-turn wait (too slow for 7-year-olds) |
| Doubles extra turn | **Off** in v1 (optional adult rule in RULES.md) | Reduces turn length variance in classroom |
| Auctions | **Off** | Per brief; unowned habitat stays with bank if passed |
| Mortgaging / trading | **Off** | Reduces rules overhead |
| Bankruptcy | Assets return to **bank**, improvements removed | Simpler than creditor negotiation |

## Too complex for 7-year-olds (deferred or simplified)

- **Full set rent math with partial ownership** — v1 uses printed visit fees; owning a full biome doubles **unimproved** base rent only.
- **Mortgage interest and unmortgage** — cut entirely.
- **Building unevenly across a biome** — must build evenly (one nest on each habitat in the biome before a second nest on any of them).
- **Negotiated deals between players** — cut; reduces social conflict in classroom.
- **Income tax percentage** — flat Visitor Center Fee (**$2**) instead.
